Project Overview

I designed an online flower shop as a solo course project focused on fast delivery of flowers, bouquets, and indoor plants.

To help beginners, I included simple care instructions with each plant, inspired by my own experience with flower care.

The design uses a soft, spring-inspired palette of green, lavender, and rose to create a fresh and warm feeling.

While I didn’t create wireframes or conduct usability tests, I focused on a clean, inviting interface.

This project taught me the importance of combining practical features with emotional design.

Project Overview

This was a team project for a fitness website selling equipment, sportswear, diet plans, and workout routines.

We aimed to attract traffic and build a community through an educational blog and fitness forum.

I collaborated on layout, design decisions, and helped create wireframes to structure the site.

We chose a fresh, energetic color palette using soft mint green, lavender purple, aqua green, black, and white.

The result is a clean, modern platform that promotes both shopping and healthy lifestyle engagement.

Project Overview

This travel app was a solo course project focused on helping users book flights, accommodations, and travel services with ease.

The goal was to create a reliable, frequently used platform that could also support profitability.

Key features included live flight updates, delay notifications, and offline maps with saved destinations and wish lists.

A vibrant palette of orange, light blue, yellow, white, and black was chosen to create energy and clarity.

The design balances functionality with a clean, user-friendly experience.

Project Overview

Pocket Weather is a simple, approachable weather app designed to clearly present weather information.

The goal was to build user trust and encourage regular use through accurate data and helpful features.

Pocket Weather stands out by offering custom user-made alerts, like “Alert me if the UV is above 10.”

The color palette red, yellow, blue, grey, black, and white represents different weather conditions while keeping the interface clean.

Overall, Pocket Weather focuses on personalization and usability for everyday forecasting.
